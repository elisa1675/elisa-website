Producer: DocStudio Malta

Location: The Malta Society of Arts, Valletta

Description

Reflects on the shared obsolescence of technology and the human condition in the digital age. Through a series of newly developed works, it invites critical engagement with the psychological, social, and environmental costs of technological progress—from the emotional toll of digital acceleration to the material consequences of planned obsolescence and e-waste.

The works on show span generative video, modified interfaces, sculptural assemblage, and algorithmic sound. Some manipulate obsolete software to create surreal, degraded landscapes; others rewire everyday devices into dysfunctional prototypes or glitch aesthetics. Throughout, physical and digital materials are entangled, printed circuit boards sit alongside brushstrokes while coded behaviours interrupt sculptural forms—blurring the boundary between technological debris and artistic gesture.

Please explore the systems that make both machines and people disposable: planned obsolescence, algorithmic burnout, fractured attention, and the quiet violence of constant upgrades. Visitors are invited to reflect on a culture where relevance is engineered to expire by design, not by necessity.
